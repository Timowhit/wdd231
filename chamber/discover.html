Discover Gilbert

From humble farming roots to a thriving modern community, Gilbert's story is one of growth, innovation, and unwavering community spirit.

Our Heritage

A Rich Agricultural Legacy

Gilbert's story begins in 1902 when the Arizona Eastern Railway established a rail line through the area. Named after railroad surveyor Bobby Gilbert, the town quickly became the agricultural heart of the East Valley.

By the 1920s, Gilbert had earned the title "Hay Shipping Capital of the World," with vast cotton and alfalfa fields stretching across the landscape. The iconic Gilbert Water Tower, built in 1926, still stands today as a beloved symbol of our heritage.

The 1980s brought transformation as Gilbert evolved from a small farming community of 5,000 residents to one of America's fastest-growing towns. Today, our population exceeds 275,000, yet we've preserved the small-town charm that makes Gilbert special.
